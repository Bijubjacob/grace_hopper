About Grace Hopper

Grace Hopper (1906-1992) was a computer scientist and U.S. Navy rear admiral. She pioneered the development of COBOL and popularized the term "debugging."

Biography

Grace Hopper was born on December 9, 1906, in New York City. She earned a Ph.D. in mathematics from Yale University and joined the U.S. Navy Reserve during World War II.

She worked on the Mark I computer at Harvard University and later developed the first compiler for a programming language, A-0.

Achievements

  • Developed the first compiler for a programming language.
  • Popularized machine-independent programming languages.
  • Received the Presidential Medal of Freedom in 2016 (posthumously).
